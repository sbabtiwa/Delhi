History

Delhi, officially known as the National Capital Territory of Delhi (NCT), has been India’s capital since 1911. It is a “union territory,” which means the city is ruled by the central (federal) government. Delhi is the second wealthiest city in India after Mumbai, with a total wealth of $450 billion and home to 18 billionaires and 23,000 millionaires.

The city has been constantly occupied since around 6 BC. It is believed to be the site of Indraprastha, the legendary capital of the Pandavas in the Indian epic Mahabharata. Delhi has served as the capital for many dynasties, including the Tughlaqs, the Khiljis, and the Mughals.

Today, Delhi can be divided into three parts:

Old Delhi: This is where one can find narrow lanes, old havelis (traditional mansions), and colorful bazaars. This part of Delhi reflects the pre-colonial time.

Central Delhi: It is known as an administrative area. Several government buildings are located here, such as the Rashtrapati Bhavan and Parliament House. The streets are lined with trees.

“New” Delhi: Connected by a well-planned rapid transit network, the Delhi Metro. There is the Nehru Stadium, which was used for the 2010 Commonwealth Games. New malls, entertainment centers, and health centers are all over the area.
