More Information



India Gate - An “Arc de Triomphe”-style gate that serves as a memorial to 70,000 members of the British Indian Army who died in the period 1914-21 in the First World War.


Red Fort – A towering Mughal fortress built in the 17th century that is made from red sandstone. It became a UNESCO World Heritage Site in 2007.


Connaught Place – A busy business and financial hub, centered on a ring of colonnaded Georgian-style buildings with international store chains, theaters, and restaurants.


Chandni Chowk – Located in the heart of Old Delhi. It is a huge market where one can purchase spices, jewelry, and colorful Indian outfits.


Qutub Minar – A 240 ft tower built in 1193 by the Delhi Sultanate. It became a UNESCO World Heritage Site in 1993.


Delhi Metro - The largest and busiest metro in India, the world’s 9th longest metro system, and the 16th largest in ridership. It has 214 stations and 7 lines.
